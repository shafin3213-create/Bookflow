The story is about Jay Gatsby and his pursuit of the American Dream in the roaring twenties era of New York. He lived in West Egg and threw lavish parties hoping to attract Daisy Buchanan who lived across the bay in East Egg. The green light across the water represented his hopes and dreams for the future. Nick Carraway narrates the story and observes the moral decay of the wealthy elite. The Valley of Ashes lies between West Egg and New York City as a symbol of the spiritual and moral decay of the society. The eyes of Doctor T J Eckleburg watch over this wasteland as a symbol of God or moral authority. Gatsby believed in the green light the orgastic future that year by year recedes before us. They were careless people Tom and Daisy they smashed up things and creatures and then retreated back into their money. So we beat on boats against the current borne back ceaselessly into the past. The Great Gatsby is a masterful critique of the American Dream exploring themes of wealth class social status and the illusion of the past.
